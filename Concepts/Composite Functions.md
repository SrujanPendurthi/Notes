If $f: A \to B$ and $g: B \to C$ are functions, then the composition of $g$ and $f$ is the function $g \circ f: A \to C$ given by $(g \circ f)(x) = g(f(x))$

Composite Functions are not Communtative

Composite Functions are associative. That is, if $f: A \to B$, $g: B \to C$, $h: C \to D$, then $h \circ (g \circ f) = (h \circ g) \circ f$

Let $f: A \to B$ be a function. Then $f \circ i_A = i_B \circ f = f$
