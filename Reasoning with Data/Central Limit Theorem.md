Sampling Distribution of a Statistic is the probability distribution of all possible values of a single statistic
	Characterized by Center, Spread, Shape

Although the values of the statistic will vary from sample to sample, there will be predictable regularities due to Law of Large Numbers(center), Central Limit Theorem(Shape), and the sigma-over-square root of sample size rule(Spread)

As long as random sampling, 
	the mean of sampling distribution = population mean 
	standard deviation = $\frac {\text{standard deviation in the population}} {\text{square root of sample size}}$
	Shape will be normal is $n \ge 30$, the higher it gets, the more normal

Statistical Significance is when an ordinary random variation alone was very unlikely to have produced the observed study effect, then the effect is probably real, and then the result is said to be statistically significant
	Claim -> Observe and Model -> Compute Probability -> Confirm
