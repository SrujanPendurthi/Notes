The idea of Statistical Independence

Two events are statistically independent if, when you know the outcome of one, that doesn't change your calculated probability for outcomes of the other.

Statistical independence with variables is when the association between A and B isn't significantly different from what would be expected just by natural chance.

 Statistically independence is defined mathematically in terms of probability in several different but equivalent ways.

Special Multiplication Rule for Statistically Independent Events: $P(A \cap B) = P(A) * P(B)$
	states the probability of two or more independent events both occurring 
Equality of Conditional Probabilities of an event$P(B|A) = P(B|A^c) = P(B)$
